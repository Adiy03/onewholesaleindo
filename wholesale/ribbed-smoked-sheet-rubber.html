HS Code: 400121 Smoked sheets of natural rubber

Ribbed smoked sheet rubber (RSS) are a natural form of processed rubber derived from the latex of rubber trees, which are widely cultivated in tropical regions.

The latex is collected, coagulated, and then rolled into thin sheets before being smoked and dried.

This smoking process not only preserves the rubber but also enhances its strength, durability, and resistance to microbial decay.

The sheets typically have a light brown color and a distinct smoky aroma, indicating high-quality processing.

One of the primary uses of smoked rubber sheets is in the manufacture of tires, footwear, rubber hoses, conveyor belts, and various industrial products.

Their high elasticity, tensile strength, and abrasion resistance make them a preferred raw material for multiple manufacturing applications.

Smoked sheets also play an important role in the production of rubber goods for automotive, construction, and engineering industries.

Their natural resilience and flexibility contribute to performance and longevity in end-use products.

Smoked Sheets of Natural Rubber are classified based on international standards known as Ribbed Smoked Sheet
  • RSS1: The highest grade, light-colored with a clean surface and free from blemishes or impurities.
  • RSS2: Slightly lower in color uniformity but maintains excellent quality and strength.
  • RSS3: The most common commercial grade, offering a balance between quality and cost efficiency.
  • RSS4: Used primarily for industrial applications where color and appearance are less critical.
  • RSS5: The lowest commercial grade, suitable for general manufacturing processes.
