Palm oil is a highly versatile commodity, widely used in food manufacturing, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and renewable energy. Its cost-effectiveness, long shelf life, and multifunctional properties make it an indispensable ingredient in the global supply chain.

Palm Oil Uses:

Crude Palm Oil (CPO): Unrefined oil extracted from the flesh of the oil palm fruit. Raw material for margarine, shortening, cooking oil, soaps, and biodiesel.

Refined, Bleached, and Deodorized Palm Oil (RBD Palm Oil): Palm oil that has been purified, decolorized, and deodorized. Used in cooking oil, processed foods, and as the base material for other palm oil derivatives.

RBD Palm Olein: The liquid fraction obtained from RBD Palm Oil. Commonly used as edible cooking oil, margarine, and in instant food products.

RBD Palm Stearin: The solid fraction from RBD Palm Oil. Used in shortening, solid margarine, candles, and bar soaps.

Palm Kernel Oil (PKO): Oil extracted from the kernel (seed) of the oil palm fruit. Widely used in cosmetics, liquid soaps, detergents, and confectionery products.

Refined Palm Kernel Oil (RPKO): Purified PKO with removed color and odor. Used in creams, lotions, ice cream, and chocolate.

Palm Kernel Expeller (also known as Palm Kernel Cake) A by-product of the palm kernel oil extraction process. After the oil is extracted from the palm kernel seed, the remaining solid material rich in fiber and protein is referred to as PKE or PKC.Widely used as animal feed due to its beneficial nutritional content.

Palm Fatty Acid Distillate (PFAD): By-product from the palm oil refining process. Used in soap manufacturing, animal feed, and biodiesel feedstock.

Biodiesel (FAME): Eco-friendly fuel made from palm oil. A renewable energy source for transportation and industrial use.
