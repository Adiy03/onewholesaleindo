Palm Fatty Acid Distillate (PFAD) is a versatile byproduct obtained during the refining process of crude palm oil, composed mainly of free fatty acids, tocopherols, and other minor lipids.

Widely used in the oleochemical, feed, and industrial sectors, it serves as an essential ingredient for soap manufacturing, biodiesel production, and animal feed formulations.

Uses and Benefits of PFAD

PFAD (Palm Fatty Acid Distillate) is a palm oil distillation by-product rich in free fatty acids, widely used in the oleochemical, animal feed, soap, biodiesel, candle, and chemical industries.

Its applications include raw material for soap noodles, surfactants, fatty alcohol, fatty acid, calcium soap for animal feed, as well as a key component in biodiesel transesterification processes.

The advantages of PFAD include its high FFA content, chemical stability, optimal melting point, and its ability to be processed into various derivative compounds.

In addition, PFAD is more cost-effective compared to refined vegetable oils, making it an economical choice for large-scale industries.

PFAD also offers good biodegradability, making it environmentally friendly and suitable for industrial applications requiring sustainable base materials.
