Palm Sugar is a natural sweetener derived from the sap of palm trees, particularly coconut and aren varieties.

Recognized for its rich caramel-like flavor, low glycemic index, and nutrient content, Palm Sugar is commonly used in food and beverage applications, including confectionery, bakery, sauces, health foods, and traditional cuisine.

Its natural origin, sustainable production methods, and consistent availability make Indonesian Palm Sugar a highly valued alternative to refined cane sugar for global food industries seeking healthier and eco-friendly sweetening solutions.

Palm / Coconut Sugar Uses:

Food & Beverage: Natural sweetener for coffee, tea, smoothies, desserts, sauces, and traditional recipes.

Bakery & Confectionery: Adds caramel-like flavor and natural sweetness to cakes, cookies, chocolates, energy bars, and candies.

Health & Wellness Products: Popular in organic, vegan, and low-glycemic foods as a healthier alternative to refined sugar.

Traditional & Asian Cuisine: Essential ingredient in Indonesian, Thai, Vietnamese, and Indian dishes for balancing sweet and savory flavors.

Beverage Industry: Used in craft drinks, kombucha, herbal beverages, and natural syrups.

Export & Retail: Supplied in powder, block, or syrup form for global markets, supporting food industries and health-conscious consumers worldwide.
