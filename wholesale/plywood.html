Plywood Types

In short, plywood is divided into the following types:
  • Interior → MR / Decorative
  • Semi-exterior (humid) → BWR
  • Exterior (outdoor & weatherproof) → Marine / Exterior Grade
  • Heavy construction → Structural
  • Premium & aesthetics → Hardwood / Veneer

Commercial Plywood (Interior Grade / MR Plywood)

MR = Moisture Resistant (not waterproof, only resistant to mild humidity).
Commonly used for indoor furniture, cabinets, shelves, and wall paneling.
Relatively cheaper compared to other types.

BWR Plywood (Boiling Water Resistant)

More water-resistant than MR grade.
Suitable for kitchen cabinets, bathroom furniture, doors, and humid Moderate durability for interior & semi-exterior use.

Marine Plywood

Highest quality, resistant to water & extreme weather.
Bonded with phenolic resin to ensure true waterproofing.
Ideal for exterior use, boats, bridges, decking, and outdoor construction.

Hardwood Plywood

Made from hardwood species such as teak, mahogany, birch, or oak.
Strong, dense, and durable with excellent load-bearing capacity.
Suitable for flooring, premium furniture, tables, shelves, and doors.

Softwood Plywood

Produced from softwood species like pine, spruce, and fir.
Lightweight, economical, and easy to work with.
Commonly used for roofing, walls, temporary flooring, and general construction.

Decorative / Veneer Plywood

Covered with luxury veneer on the surface for a premium look.
Provides aesthetic appeal and smooth finishing.
Ideal for cabinets, tables, wall paneling, and decorative interior design.

Flexible Plywood (Flexi Ply)

Thin, lightweight, and highly bendable for curved designs.
Easily shaped into unique architectural and interior applications.
Perfect for furniture with curves, interior decoration, and creative designs.

Structural Plywood

Designed for heavy-duty construction and load-bearing applications.
Offers excellent strength and pressure resistance.
Widely used in concrete formwork, scaffolding, flooring, and building projects.

Exterior Grade Plywood

Specially manufactured for outdoor applications.
Resistant to moisture, heat, and changing weather conditions.
Used in cladding, fencing, roofing, and exterior furniture.
