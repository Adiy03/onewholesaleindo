Palm Kernel Shell (PKS) is a hard, fibrous by product obtained from the processing of palm oil, widely available in Indonesia as one of the world’s largest palm producers.

Valued for its high calorific energy, low ash content, and stable supply, PKS is extensively used in biomass power plants, industrial boilers, briquette and pellet production, as well as raw material for charcoal and activated carbon.

Its eco-friendly profile, cost-effectiveness, and contribution to renewable energy make Palm Kernel Shell from Indonesia a preferred alternative fuel source for global industries.

Palm Kernel Shell (PKS) Uses:

Biomass Power Generation: PKS is widely used as a renewable fuel for biomass power plants due to its high calorific value and low ash content, making it an eco-friendly alternative to coal.

Industrial Boilers: Commonly used in cement, textile, food, and other industries as a cost-effective solid fuel for steam and heat generation.

Charcoal & Activated Carbon Production: PKS can be carbonized into charcoal and processed into activated carbon, which is in high demand for water and air purification.

Briquettes & Pellets: Crushed PKS is often compacted into briquettes or pellets as biomass fuel for household and industrial applications.

Soil Amendment & Compost: In agriculture, PKS ash is sometimes utilized as a soil conditioner and nutrient-rich composting material.
