Charcoal for Sale: Indonesia Wholesale Trade

HS Code: 4402XX Charcoal

Charcoal is a solid, carbon-rich material produced through the controlled carbonization of natural biomass, such as wood or coconut shells.

It is widely used for barbecues (bbq), grilling, household cooking, heating, metallurgy, and activated carbon production, making it an essential energy source for both domestic and industrial applications.

Indonesia Charcoal Types:

1. Wood Lump Charcoal

Applications: Household BBQ, restaurants, outdoor cooking.

Wood Lump Charcoal is produced by carbonizing hardwood under controlled conditions, resulting in irregular but natural-shaped pieces.

Known for its high calorific value, it ignites quickly and delivers intense heat. Its smoky aroma enhances food flavor, making it a favorite for traditional BBQ lovers.

2. Coconut Shell Charcoal

Applications: Hookah/Shisha, BBQ, activated carbon production.

Made from carefully selected coconut shells, this charcoal is renowned for its density, hardness, and eco-friendly profile.

It burns hotter and longer than wood charcoal while producing very little smoke and ash.

Because of its clean and consistent performance, it is widely exported for shisha, BBQ, and as a raw material for activated carbon.

3. Charcoal Briquettes

Applications: BBQ, household cooking, industrial fuel.

Charcoal Briquettes are manufactured by compressing charcoal fines with natural binders into uniform shapes such as pillow, cube, or hexagonal.

They are designed for efficiency, providing long-lasting heat with consistent size and quality.

Ideal for large-scale use, they are easy to package and transport, making them one of the most popular choices for export markets.

4. Extruded Charcoal (Stick/Bar Charcoal)

Applications: Professional BBQ, restaurants, shisha/hookah. Extruded Charcoal is machine-pressed into stick or hexagonal form, often featuring a hollow center for better airflow.

This design allows for stable burning, long duration, and very minimal ash residue. Its clean handling and consistent performance make it a preferred option for professional kitchens, restaurants, and shisha lounges.

5. Bamboo Charcoal

Applications: Health products, air and water purification, clean-burning fuel.

Bamboo Charcoal is produced by carbonizing mature bamboo, resulting in a lightweight yet highly porous charcoal. Its micro-porous structure makes it excellent for odor absorption, moisture control, and air purification.

Beyond industrial uses, it also provides steady burning with low smoke, making it a versatile eco-friendly product.

6. Palm Kernel Shell Charcoal (PKS Charcoal)

Applications: Boilers, power generation, industrial heating.

PKS Charcoal is derived from palm kernel shells, a byproduct of the palm oil industry. It is a sustainable and cost-effective biomass fuel with high energy content.

This charcoal is increasingly used as an alternative to coal for boilers, power plants, and industrial furnaces, supporting renewable energy initiatives worldwide.

7. Activated Charcoal (Activated Carbon)

Applications:Water and air filtration, medical, cosmetics.

Activated Charcoal undergoes special high-temperature processing to create a highly porous structure with an exceptional surface area.

These properties make it one of the best adsorbents for impurities, chemicals, and toxins. Trusted globally, it plays a vital role in water treatment, air purification, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetic industries.

8. Binchotan (White Charcoal)

Applications:Fine dining BBQ, water purification, specialty exports.

Binchotan, also known as White Charcoal, is a premium charcoal fired at extremely high temperatures.

It is recognized for its unique hardness, clean burning, and almost smokeless performance.

Often used in fine dining BBQ and specialty exports, it is also applied in water purification and health products due to its mineral-rich properties.
