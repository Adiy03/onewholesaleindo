Buy Bulk Wood Charcoal from Indonesia

HS Code: 4402XX Wood charcoal

Charcoal is a natural solid fuel produced through the carbonization of wood, coconut shells, or other biomass materials.

Renowned for its high calorific value, long burning duration, and cleaner combustion, charcoal is available in both traditional lump forms and processed varieties such as briquettes.

It is widely used for barbecues, grilling, household cooking, heating, metallurgy, and activated carbon production, making it an essential energy source for both domestic and industrial applications.

Wood Charcoal Uses:

Barbecues & Grilling: Charcoal is the preferred fuel for barbecues and grilling, producing steady high heat and adding a distinctive smoky flavor to food.

Household Cooking: In many countries, charcoal remains an essential energy source for daily cooking, especially in traditional stoves and open-fire methods.

Heating: Charcoal provides consistent and long-lasting heat, making it suitable for home heating, restaurants, and small-scale commercial uses.

Industrial Applications: Widely used in metallurgy, blacksmithing, ceramics, and other industries that require intense and reliable heat for production processes.

Activated Carbon Production: High-quality charcoal is the primary raw material for activated carbon, which is used in water purification, air filtration, gold recovery, and pharmaceuticals.

Agricultural & Artisanal Uses: Charcoal can be applied as biochar to improve soil fertility and is also used in cosmetics, incense, and natural absorbents.

Types of Charcoal:

a. Traditional Lump Charcoal

Natural charcoal made directly from wood carbonization without additional binders or processing.
  • Hardwood Lump Charcoal – Produced from dense tropical hardwoods such as mangrove, acacia, mahogany, and tamarind. Known for high heat, long burning, and low ash.
  • Binchotan (White Charcoal) – A premium Japanese-style charcoal, extremely dense, burns at very high temperatures, and produces almost no smoke or odor.
  • Coconut Shell Charcoal (Lump Form) – Made from carbonized coconut shells, burns cleanly, produces minimal smoke, and is highly valued for eco-friendliness.

b. Processed Charcoal (Briquettes & Agglomerated Forms)

Charcoal that is crushed, mixed with natural binders (such as tapioca starch), and compressed into uniform shapes.
  • Sawdust Briquettes – Produced from compressed sawdust charcoal powder; offers steady heat, long burning, and uniform size.
  • Coconut Shell Briquettes – Popular for shisha/hookah and barbecues due to clean burn, low ash, and odorless performance.
  • Mixed Biomass Briquettes – A blend of wood, shells, and other biomass, designed for cost-effectiveness and versatile fuel use.
