HS Code: 281410 Anhydrous ammonia

Anhydrous Ammonia NH3 is a colorless gas with a pungent odor, widely recognized for its high nitrogen content and crucial role as a feedstock in fertilizer and industrial chemical production.

Produced through the Haber-Bosch process by combining hydrogen and nitrogen under high pressure and temperature, anhydrous ammonia offers exceptional purity and consistency for large-scale industrial applications.

It is extensively used across various sectors, including agriculture, refrigeration, water treatment, and the chemical industry, serving as a fundamental raw material for urea, nitric acid, and other nitrogen-based compounds.

In addition, anhydrous ammonia is available in bulk and cylinder packaging options, ensuring safe storage, handling, and efficient delivery for both agricultural and industrial needs. visual elegance.

Function: Serves as a primary nitrogen source, essential for the production of urea, ammonium nitrate, and other nitrogen-based fertilizers that enhance soil fertility and crop yield.

Function: Acts as a key raw material in the production of nitric acid, amines, and various chemical intermediates used across multiple industrial processes.

How to Use: NH3 Supplied in pressurized tanks for use in reactors or chemical synthesis systems under regulated temperature and pressure conditions.
