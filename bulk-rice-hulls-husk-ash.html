Rice Hulls & Rice Husk Ash


Rice hull, also known as rice husk, is the hard outer shell of rice grains removed during milling. It is rich in organic fiber and carbon, making it suitable for livestock feed, eco-friendly biomass fuel, and industrial raw materials. Our rice hulls (husk) availlable:


Rice Husk, which is the hard outer shell of rice grains, rich in organic fiber. With an organic carbon content of approximately 30–50%, it is widely used for livestock feed, biomass fuel, and eco-friendly industrial materials.


Rice Husk Powder, a finely ground form of husk with a flour-like texture. It serves multiple purposes such as adhesive for plywood, insect repellent coils, and other industrial chemical mixtures.


Rice Husk Dust is an ultra-fine, lightweight powder derived from ground husk. With porous and insulating properties, it is commonly used as a filler material, thermal insulation, and soil enhancer in agriculture.

Rice Husk Ash (RHA), a combustion by-product of rice husk with high silica content. RHA is available in three variants based on carbon and silica levels:


  • Low (≤ 2.5% carbon, ≥ 90% silica)
  • Normal (5–15% carbon, ≥ 80% silica)
  • High (≥ 40% carbon, ≤ 60% silica)
